The first version of an AI tool is usually the easiest part. The hard part starts when the system has to keep working after the prompt changes, the model changes, or the input distribution shifts.

These are working notes on the habits I want to build while turning demos into systems.

## What reliability means

For me, reliability starts with a few practical questions:

- Can I see when the system is wrong?
- Can I replay examples after changing the implementation?
- Can I explain why one version is better than another?
- Can I keep the system small enough to understand?

## What I want to practice

The goal is not to make every project enterprise-grade. The goal is to practice the engineering moves that make AI systems less mysterious:

- Clear inputs and outputs.
- Small evaluation sets.
- Logs that explain failures.
- Versioned prompts and model choices.
- Interfaces that expose uncertainty instead of hiding it.

That is the difference between a demo that impresses once and a system that teaches you something.
