Most "AI projects" you can find online are demos. They demonstrate that a model exists and that the author can call its API. They do not demonstrate engineering taste.

This is a working note on what I am trying to optimize for when I pick projects for myself.

## What a demo proves

A demo proves that a thing runs. It does not prove that:

- The author understands when the thing fails.
- The author thought about evaluation.
- The author would catch a silent regression after a model upgrade.
- The system is cheap enough to keep running.

## What I want a project to prove

When I look back at a project a year later, I want it to have left me with:

- A clearer sense of where a class of system breaks.
- An evaluation harness I can re-run.
- A simpler mental model than I had before starting.
- At least one decision I now disagree with — that is how I know I learned.

Demos are fine. They just are not the point.
